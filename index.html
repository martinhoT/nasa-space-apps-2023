Everything Starts with Water

Let's Start at The Beginning


How did the oceans come about?
Scientists have a theory that the first ocean was formed from the escape of water vapor and other gases from the molten rocks of the Earth to the atmosphere surrounding the cooling planet.
After the Earth's surface had cooled to a temperature below the boiling point of water, rain began to fall—and continued to fall for centuries. As the water drained into the great hollows in the Earth's surface, the primeval ocean came into existence. The forces of gravity prevented the water from leaving the planet.
There is also another more interesting theory, that says that comets and meteorites that crashed against Earth brought the water to our oceans. Imagine how cool is that!



Why is water so important for us?
Water is essential to life on Earth. In its three phases (solid, liquid, and gas), water ties together the major parts of the Earth’s climate system — air, clouds, the ocean, lakes, vegetation, snowpacks and glaciers.
A process called the water cycle is what allows the continuous movement of water within the Earth and atmosphere. It is a complex system that includes many different processes that we’re excited to teach you!

The solutions


We can stop things from getting worse!



Reduce pollution
Sea levels are rising because of pollution from things like cars and factories. These pollutions make the Earth warmer, which melts ice and makes the sea level go up.



Use clean energy
Instead of using things like coal and oil, which make lots of pollution, we can use clean energy like the sun and the wind to make electricity.



Save energy
We use less energy, we produce less pollution.



Plant trees
Trees are like nature's cleaners. They absorb carbon dioxide, which is a gas that makes the Earth warmer when there's too much of it in the air.



Recycle and reduce waste
When we recycle our trash and use less plastic, we reduce the amount of garbage that ends up in the ocean.




Remember, even small actions can add up to a big impact. So, by doing these things, we can help protect our planet.
